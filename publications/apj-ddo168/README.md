# Modeling Solitonic Cores, Stabilization of Bar, and Suppression of Bar Dissolution in DDO 168 via GPP Formalism

## Theoretical Framework and Supporting Materials

This directory contains the supporting materials for my first-author publication in *The Astrophysical Journal*, which presents a theoretical investigation of **Bose–Einstein Condensate (BEC)/Fuzzy Dark Matter** in the dwarf irregular galaxy **DDO 168**.

The study develops a theoretical framework based on the **Gross–Pitaevskii–Poisson (GPP) formalism** to investigate how solitonic dark matter cores influence the internal dynamics of dwarf galaxies. By combining analytical modeling with observational constraints, the work explores the relationship between halo structure, galaxy rotation curves, stellar bar stabilization, and the suppression of bar dissolution.

This repository is intended to improve the accessibility and reproducibility of the research by providing supporting materials related to the publication.

---

## Research Overview

The primary objectives of this work are to:

* Develop a theoretical GPP-based framework for modeling the dark matter halo of DDO 168.
* Investigate the formation and physical properties of solitonic core structures.
* Examine the influence of Bose–Einstein Condensate/Fuzzy Dark Matter on stellar bar stability.
* Explore the suppression of bar dissolution through dark matter halo dynamics.
* Compare theoretical predictions with observational properties of DDO 168.
